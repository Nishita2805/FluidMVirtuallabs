Experiment 5: Flow Over a Notch Experiment

Aim

To study and determine the flow over a notch, including the discharge coefficient for V-notch and rectangular notch.

Apparatus Required

  • Notch apparatus with V-notch and rectangular notch
  • Measuring tank
  • Stopwatch
  • Scale or gauge

Theory

Flow over a notch is used to measure discharge in open channels. The theoretical discharge can be calculated using the following formulas:

For V-Notch:

Qv = (8/15) × √(2g) × tan(θ/2) × H2.5

For Rectangular Notch:

Qr = (2/3) × √(2g) × B × H1.5

Where Q is the discharge, g is the acceleration due to gravity, θ is the angle of the notch, B is the breadth of the notch, and H is the head over the notch.

Procedure

  1. Set up the notch apparatus and ensure it is level.
  2. Pour water into the tank and allow it to flow through the notch.
  3. Measure the head (H) over the notch using the gauge.
  4. Record the time taken to fill a known volume in the measuring tank using a stopwatch.
  5. Calculate the actual discharge (Qactual) from the measured values.
  6. Use the formulas to calculate the theoretical discharge for both V-notch and rectangular notch.
  7. Determine the coefficient of discharge (Cd) using the formula:

    8. Cd = Qactual / Qtheoretical
